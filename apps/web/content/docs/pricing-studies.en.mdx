## Study types

When creating a new study from the **Pricing** section, choose one of three types:

| Type | When to use it |
|------|----------------|
| **Full study** | From quantity takeoff to final price — most accurate for tenders |
| **Cost pricing** | Manual tables without calculation engines — when you already have quantities |
| **Quick pricing** | Straight to the selling price — for urgent offers |

## Study stages

A full study goes through four stages; each is approved before moving to the next:

**Quantities → Specifications → Cost Pricing → Selling Price**

## Structural quantity takeoff

1. Start with the **Building Setup Wizard**: define floors, heights, and areas — the system automatically derives column and blockwork heights.
2. Enter element dimensions across the eight sections:
   - Blinding concrete
   - Footings (isolated, combined, strip, raft)
   - Ground beams
   - Columns and column necks
   - Slabs (solid, hordi, flat slab)
   - Block walls
   - Stairs
   - Other elements (tanks, septic pits, elevator pits...)
3. For every element the system instantly calculates **concrete volume, detailed rebar weight, formwork area, and block count** — using formulas based on the Saudi Building Code with adjustable defaults.

## Finishing and MEP

- **Finishing:** the system derives plastering, paint, tiling, and ceiling quantities from building data and room types — every item is editable.
- **MEP:** derives electrical, plumbing, and HVAC points per room profiles.
- **Manual:** add any custom items in the manual entry tab.

## Quantity summary (BOQ)

An aggregate table with three tabs: **Summary** across all sections, **Factory Order** for rebar by diameter and length, and **Cutting Details** to minimize bar waste — with per-floor filtering and Excel/PDF export.

## Cost pricing and margins

After approving quantities and specifications, price materials and labor, then add profit margin using one of four methods:

1. A **uniform percentage** across the whole study.
2. A **percentage per section** (structural / finishing / MEP).
3. A **manual selling price** per item.
4. A **price per square meter**.

## Converting to a quotation

After approving the pricing stage, the **Convert to Quotation** button appears — it copies the items into a professional branded quotation ready to send, and when you win the tender you convert the quotation into an actual project with no re-entry.
